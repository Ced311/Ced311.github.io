Your adventure starts from the perspective of a young warrior with a fire in their heart for new experiences. You find yourself dwelling in a small town by the name of Place. After a time of recuperation in this town after your last mission, you decide to set out once more to your next awaited objective; that being to make it to the large city of Cities just north of here. This cities resources are a necessity on your journey. However, the paths to get there are very high risk without an air transport. Two branching pathways. one that brings you to the eastern gate of the city via a treaturous montain pass, and another that brings you to western gate by way of a dense forest. Which path will you choose?

You decide to take the eastern route and head out with viger, bidding your recently met aquaintances in the village farwell as you leave Place. You set out in the early morning and your trek for the most part is very peaceful, aside from some very minor monsters, that even the weakest of warriors could handle. As you continue your journey you notice the scenery start to shift significantly. The once rolling foot hills surrounding Place now transition to a much more level and heavily vegetated ground. The clear skies now obstructed by large overbearing trees. By midday you are completely surrounded by forest. You finally come to a break in the tree line and see a small interwoven bridge that follow a river downstream. It will be a clear shot to the city from this point. However it is said to around this season, dangerous aquatic monsters become more aggressive along this route, knowing that possible prey frequents it. You have two choices. Either follow the river, and take the risk in exchange for faster travel, or find another route at a distance from the river, but with it still in view to not lose your direction.

You decide take the western route is best. You head out with ambition, having thoroghly prepared yourself for the adventure. You leave early, slightly before sunrise, to have the maximum amount of daylight, as you plan to make this a one day journey, arriving to the city by dusk. Your path starts out simple enough. As you proceed you are attacked by the common place monsters that surrond Place. They are nothing you can't handle, and you dispatch them with ease. As time progresses the foothill start to raise in altitude with every mile until soon you find yourself treking through full on mountain ranges. Now midday the sky has become overcast and you find it hard to key visibility on your path due to a combination of heavy cloud cover, high altitude, and the recently added sulphur in the air. You've now reached the most dangerous portion of the pass, that your aquaintances back at the village had warned you about. This area is filled to the brim with dangerous monsters and volcanic elements that a warrior must be extremely cautious of. You have two choices. Either continue down the tedious yet physically clear path with low visibility, or climb to a higher altitude off the path to try to get a better feel for your surroundings.

You decide to continue down the designated path of the river with caution. After a while of treading the enviornment, you start to get a feel for it. A few aquatic beasts, a step ahead of the monsters back at Place in terms of raw power try to abuse you. With your advanced combat skills, you are able to finish them off with not much more than a few scratches and scrapes. However, your main weapon of choice has blunted severely from striking their thick and scaly hides. Finally the path starts to become more managable and the foliage starts to disapated. The river levels out. Sunset hits, and with about 30 minutes more of travel, You finally see it. The large eastern gate, holding the vast city of graces your vision , and a tinge of joy and releif populates your visage. Congrats, you've finally made it.

You decide to take your chances and remain on the path ahead. You place a cloth over your mouth and nose, and put on a conviently bought pair of googles to relieve the effects of the sulphur. You tread with advanced caution. watching your every step as not to be overtaken by magma sink holes and abuse predators. Things seem to be going fairly well. Until suddenly you hear a gutteral roar concealed in smog ahead of you. You quickly take cover. Before you can fully conceal yourself a magma geyser erupts near you place of refuge. You bound back athletically managing to avoid the immediate danger , but the impending danger has caught sight of you. you turn towards the treat but before you can properly react, in an instant you are aggresively pinned to the ground, in the clutches of excessively large claws. You feel a sharp pain piercing your right arm. A puddle of crimson, rolls down your torso, and you feel a upsurge of liquid pooling into your throat. You try to yell in an attempt to relieve the suelturing pain now populating various points of your body, but nothing comes out. You look directly at your assalaint, and what you see terrifies you to your core. A large reptilian creature with a bloodlust filled glare, a maw of flesh rending teeth, and a crown of two distinguised horns. Stands over your near corpse-like vessel, skewering your chest and right arm with its large claws. Your last bit of survival instincts kick into high gear, and with your free left arm you attempt you reach for your treasured sword hilted at your side. You have just enough range for a promising swing towards the creatures neck. You gather every bit of energy let that you can muster, finally let out a battle cry, and quickly send the attack to its intended destination. The monster slightly moves its neck and in a flash, The exact opposite of your intentions come to fruition. Your treasure sword now cleaved in two. The monster opens its maw, letting out an aggrivated roar, then aims its razor like teeth directly at your face. The last thing you hear is a loud crunch. as your vision is overtaken by the creatures maw. Your adventure has ended.
